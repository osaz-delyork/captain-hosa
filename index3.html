COUNTDOWN TO LAST FLIGHT

In what can only be described as a self-fulfilling prophecy, the incredible and exceptional story of Captain Idahosa Wells Okunbo gives us a reason to wholeheartedly celebrate his life, rather than mark the inevitability with a downcast spirit.

ABOUT

History is filled with great men, but only a few of them will be remembered for generations because they made an indelible mark. One of such men is the pre-eminent nobleman, Captain Idahosa Wells Okunbo, vindicated by his story, life and legacy.

In a lifetime filled with the daring adventure of an explorer, Captain Hosa ventured through many career paths and became a behemoth in all of these, from an accomplished commercial pilot by the young age of 30 years to become a globally recognised business magnate, investor, philanthropist and socio-economic reformer.

He took his love and belief in humanity to an ultimate expression of sacrifice and hope. His success blueprint documents resilience and discipline as its watchwords, bearing testimony to the fruits of a life of labour, faith and service to humanity.

1. The Airport, Benin.

Arrive in the beautiful and ancient city of Benin at the Benin Airport.

The local airport is situated in the heart of Benin City the capital of Edo State in Nigeria. The runway is in the middle of the city.

The airport in many ways was Captain Hosa’s first professional home, where he served two years at Okada Air and another three years with Intercontinental Airlines as a commercial pilot.

COVID-19 PROTOCOLS

COVID-19 protocols will be strictly enforced throughout the events.

WEAR A FACEMASK

Wear a mask over your nose and mouth at all times. You can cough or sneeze into your mask. Put on a new, clean mask as soon as possible and wash your hands.

SOCIAL DISTANCING

Stay at least 6 feet (about 2 arm lengths) apart from other people.

WASHING OF HANDS REGULARLY

Wash your hands with soap and water for at least 20 seconds, especially after you have been in a public place, or after blowing your nose, coughing, or sneezing.

AVOID TOUCHING OF YOUR FACE

Avoid touching your eyes, nose, and mouth with unwashed hands.

USE ALCOHOL-BASED SANITIZER

If you don’t get to wash your hands during the events, ensure you use alcohol-based sanitizer.

MONITOR YOUR HEALTH

If you have a fever, cough, shortness of breath, or other symptoms of COVID-19 during the events, please ensure you isolate yourself.
