BIOGRAPHY

History is filled with great men, but only a few of them will be remembered for generations because they made indelible marks that ascended great heights of achievement. One of such men is the pre-eminent nobleman, Captain Idahosa Wells Okunbo, who is vindicated by his productive life and inspiring legacy.

A discerning onlooker is bound to note the lifetime of a daring adventurer, an explorer who experienced and undertook his life’s journey courageously. Captain Hosa ventured through many career paths and excelled in all of them. Taking off as a commercial pilot in his early twenties, he had risen by sheer determination to be a Captain by his mid-twenties. At the young age of 30, he started on his path to becoming a globally recognized business magnate, investor, philanthropist, and socio-economic reformer. His incredible successes across multiple competitive sectors are indelible marks and remarkable legacy symbols to future generations.

He was caring and exceptionally benevolent. Cappy took his love and belief in humanity to the ultimate expression of sacrifice and hope. His extraordinary life is a blueprint of resilience, discipline, and tenacity as it bears testimony to the fruits of labour, faith, and service to humanity.

4. The Wells Hosa Greenhouse Farm

The Wells Hosa Greenhouse Farms is the first commercially successful indigenously owned greenhouse farm in Nigeria. The high-tech hydroponic greenhouse project is Captain Hosa's Legacy for the Edo people. Its mission is to nourish local communities with safe, nutritious, and delicious food.

COVID-19 PROTOCOLS

COVID-19 protocols will be strictly enforced throughout the events.

WEAR A FACEMASK

Wear a mask over your nose and mouth at all times. You can cough or sneeze into your mask. Put on a new, clean mask as soon as possible and wash your hands.

SOCIAL DISTANCING

Stay at least 6 feet (about 2 arm lengths) apart from other people.

WASHING OF HANDS REGULARLY

Wash your hands with soap and water for at least 20 seconds, especially after being in a public place or blowing your nose, coughing, or sneezing.

AVOID TOUCHING OF YOUR FACE

Avoid touching your eyes, nose, and mouth with unwashed hands.

USE ALCOHOL-BASED SANITIZER

If you are unable to wash your hands during the course of events, do ensure you use an alcohol-based sanitizer and wash your hands as soon as it becomes possible.

MONITOR YOUR HEALTH

If you have a fever, cough, shortness of breath, or other symptoms of COVID-19 during the events, please ensure you isolate yourself.
